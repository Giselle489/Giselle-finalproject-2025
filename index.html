Rules and Consequences
  1. One foot must always be in contact with the ground
  2. The advancing leg must be straight from the point of contact until the body passes over it.
  3. Judges cannot use technology like binoculars or mirrors.

Consequences

  • Time penalties (added time)
  • Disqualification

Example

The rule often broken is the loss of contact with the ground which can lead to disqualification.

Dangers and injuries

Maintaining your leg straight for this sport can lead to injuries, These injuries consist of shin splints, hamstring, knee pain etc.

Injured Athlete

  • Jared Tallent's hamstring injury caused him to miss out on the 2020 tokyo olympics
speed walking techniques

Skills to work on

  • Stand tall. No slouch in the shoulders, forward lean from the waist, or excess sway in your back.
  • Push off of your toes.
  • Swing your arms in a pendulum motion, bent at the elbows at a 90-degree angle.
